# June Section Evening: Andy Buchanan's 133 Peak Challenge

![](/images/trips/3b73ee2d-0292-2ec5-7672-987379e3ea84-jpg)  
Join us for Andy Buchanan’s talk at our next section night on Thursday 5 June.  
  
On a clear morning, Christchurch residents have an awesome view across the Canterbury plains to the peaks of the Southern Alps. The view changes dramatically from summer to winter depending on the weather.  
  
Andy Buchanan’s self-imposed retirement challenge was to climb all 133 named peaks on a hand-drawn panorama sketch published in the Christchurch Press over 50 years ago. The panorama extends from Mt Peel and Fox peak in the south to Tapuaenuku and Manukau in the north (visible from Wellington), including many peaks in the Southern Alps and the Canterbury foothills, as far away as Mt Cook and Mt Rolleston.  
![](/images/trips/79fb9616-a20c-cb53-c9e2-d2b464b34da1-jpg)  
Andy has now climbed all these peaks, and has written a book about the views, the mountains, their history, and the climbs. This talk will give information on the panorama, the peaks, and the book, with stories and photos of successes and failures along the way, with access by foot, on skis, MTB and 4WD, with many different friends.  
![](/images/trips/f3300bbd-4ea9-a03e-a431-d906668a5f9e-jpg)  
Andy is a semi-retired structural engineer. He taught civil engineering at the University of Canterbury for many years. More information about the book, the peaks, and the panorama challenge is available at [http://www.panorama.nz](http://www.panorama.nz/)  
  
As always we'll have pizza and chats, everyone is welcome to our section nights. See you there!
