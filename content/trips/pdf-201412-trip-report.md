Te Ao Whekere 7-9 November Jordan Poste Since attending the Snowcraft course and the Whangaehu hut re-opening on Ruapehu my partner and I were pumped to get out and take on another Alpine challenge. This came in the form of a peak called Te Ao Whekere, the second highest peak in the seaward Kaikouras at 2,590m. Dan Pringle stepped up as the trip leader and got the ball rolling on this mission and he was joined by Vincent Zintzen as the assistant trip leader.  The other participants that rounded out the group were Sophie, Richard, Cécile, Helen, Jenna and myself. The trip started with a ferry breakdown forcing our departure from Wellington to be delayed a couple hours.
